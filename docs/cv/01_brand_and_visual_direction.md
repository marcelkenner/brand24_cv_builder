# Brand And Visual Direction

This document expands the positioning rules from `docs/project_description.md` sections 1, 2, and 27.

## Core Objective

The CV must present the candidate as polished, credible, ambitious, detail-oriented, and executive-ready. The result should feel modern, clean, formal, and sharp, with no decorative elements that compete with content.

## Primary Impression

The design must communicate:

- professional maturity
- composure
- confidence without aggression
- precision
- discipline
- executive presence
- trustworthiness
- strong presentation standards

If a design choice weakens any of those signals, reject it.

## Personality Rules

The visual language should stay:

- modern classic
- minimal
- structured
- restrained
- premium
- editorial rather than playful
- corporate rather than creative-agency
- sharp rather than soft
- confident rather than flashy

The page should feel intentional and controlled, not expressive for its own sake.

## Role Positioning

This direction is optimized for:

- consulting
- finance
- business development
- account management
- commercial leadership
- operations
- strategy
- legal-adjacent corporate roles
- senior corporate individual contributor roles
- manager-level roles

The styling should still remain broad enough for general white-collar applications. Avoid visual choices that narrow the document into a startup, creative, or highly casual market.

## Non-Negotiable Avoid List

Do not use:

- bright saturated colors
- multiple accent colors
- infographic skill charts
- progress bars
- timeline gimmicks
- decorative icon systems
- asymmetrical experimental layouts
- exaggerated geometry
- textured backgrounds
- gradients
- shadows
- glassmorphism
- section color blocks
- monograms or oversized initials
- novelty fonts
- circular headshots

These are not “optional extras to avoid when possible.” They are outside the intended design language.

## Creative Direction Sentence

Use this sentence as the fast alignment check during implementation and review:

Design a modern-classic executive CV with a clean white base, charcoal typography, a restrained burgundy accent, disciplined spacing, subtle hierarchy, and optional top-right rectangular headshot placement, so the overall impression is polished, confident, credible, and corporate.

## Review Heuristics

Use these checks before approving any visual iteration:

- At a glance, the name and hierarchy should feel stronger than any decorative element.
- The page should look like a serious corporate document, not a portfolio layout.
- Accent usage should feel deliberate and rare.
- The document should still look premium if printed in grayscale.
- The visual tone should support an executive or manager-level application without feeling stiff or old-fashioned.
